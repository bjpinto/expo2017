Speaker Talks: Engineering in the Future and Engineering on Mars

By: Dr. Ralph Fehr and Professor Antonio Paris

Ralph Fehr was born in Reading, Pennsylvania in 1961. He earned his B.S.E.E. degree from the Pennsylvania State University at University Park in 1983, his M. E. degree in electrical engineering, concentrated in power systems, from the University of Colorado at Boulder in 1987, and his Ph. D. in electrical engineering from the University of South Florida at Tampa in 2005.

Dr. Fehr has worked in electric power engineering capacities for numerous organizations, including Gilbert / Commonwealth Engineers & Consultants, Public Service Company of New Mexico, the United States Air Force, Florida Power Corporation, and Tampa Electric Company. He has also taught a wide variety of power engineering short courses worldwide. He currently serves on the engineering faculty of the University of South Florida.

He has authored magazine articles for trade publications, and Industrial Power Distribution – 2nd Edition (Wiley / IEEE Press, 2016). Research interests include power system planning methods and system reliability enhancement techniques, infrastructure design improvements, and high‐power semiconductor applications at medium voltages.

Dr. Fehr was selected by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) from the 12 sections in the Florida Council, representing over 9,500 members, and named Outstanding Engineering Educator for 2009. Then in 2010, he was awarded the Joseph M. Biedenbach award for excellence in engineering education by IEEE Region 3. He also received the 2014 IEEE Power and Energy Society Florida West Coast Chapter Outstanding Engineer award, and was selected as Engineer of the Year by the Florida West Coast Section of IEEE in 2016. He is a registered professional engineer in Florida and New Mexico.
